# SocioScheduler Business Plan

_Date: February 7, 2026_ _Version: 1.2 Draft_ _Overview_: This is a standalone plan for SocioScheduler, a passive AI-powered social media scheduler SaaS. It stands on its own as a profitable product targeting $1K–$2K MRR ASAP, while dogfooding MuseKit. The structure is designed to be cloned for future SaaS launches: rewrite product-specific sections (e.g., personas, USPs, marketing), keep infrastructure assumptions (e.g., MuseKit features) the same. Updates in v1.2: Added section 11 for lessons learned and duplication notes, as a placeholder to track inaugural process insights and guide future clones.

## 1. Vision & Strategy

**Vision Statement**: SocioScheduler enables busy solopreneurs, small business owners, and side-hustlers to maintain a consistent, engaging social presence without ongoing effort, turning social media into a passive lead-gen tool. By launch + 2 months, it will generate $1K–$2K in stable MRR, proving the model for quick, passive revenue while hardening MuseKit for future clones.

**Core Strategy**:

- **Product Focus**: True "set it and forget it" automation via AI agents — users connect once, provide brand prefs, then get auto-trend monitoring, content gen, scheduling/posting, and alerts.
- **Market Entry**: Niche on underserved non-tech users (e.g., plumbers, realtors, gig workers like magicians or dog trainers) who need simple social boosts for local/part-time leads, avoiding direct fights with giants like Buffer.
- **Growth Approach**: Bootstrapped, organic-first (X/IndieHackers, FB groups, self-dogfooding for promotion). Stabilize at $1K–$2K MRR, then shift focus to cloning new products.
- **Passivity Principle**: Maximize automation (n8n/BullMQ/Grok) to require <5 hours/mo founder time post-launch.
- **Milestones**: MVP launch in 1–3 weeks; beta signups (100+); $1K MRR in Month 1–2; organic growth via self-posting.

## 2. Business Plan

### 2.1 Customer Personas & Market Segmentation

**Market Size**: Global social media management tools market ~$15B (2026 est.), with underserved segment for small/local biz and gig workers (10M+ US solos/SMBs per Census data; gig economy blending into side-hustles for 44% of pros per Canva 2025 report). Focus: Users who post inconsistently due to time constraints, including part-timers seeking low-tech, FB-like simplicity.

**Primary Segments**:

- **Online Solopreneurs/Creators** (40% target): Coaches, freelancers, podcasters. Pain: Burnout from manual posting. Tech-savvy: Medium.
- **Offline Small Businesses** (30% target): Plumbers, realtors, trainers, local shops. Pain: Need leads but hate social tech. Tech-savvy: Low.
- **Side-Hustlers/Gig Workers** (30% target): Magicians, dog trainers, vending renters, landlords, house flippers, cleaners, moonlight electricians. Pain: Casual lead-gen without full business commitment; want tools as easy as scrolling FB/Pinterest. Tech-savvy: Very low.

**Key Personas**:

- **Realtor Rita** (45, solo agent): Posts market tips sporadically; wants auto-reactions to local trends (e.g., "housing boom mentions") for leads. Budget: $30–$70/mo.
- **Plumber Paul** (52, small owner): Zero social game; needs simple setup to share service tips/respond to weather events. Budget: $20–$50/mo; values zero maintenance.
- **Coach Carla** (32, online solopreneur): Consistent but time-poor; wants brand-aligned auto-posts on viral challenges. Budget: $40–$80/mo.
- **Gig Gary** (38, moonlighting electrician/magician): Scans FB for jobs but misses opportunities; wants auto-posts on "electrician needed" mentions without tech hassle. Budget: $20–$50/mo.

Focus: Direct/indirect players in AI schedulers for small users. Analyzed via 2026 reviews (e.g., G2, Capterra, X threads on "best passive scheduler for small biz/side hustles").

### 2.3 Detailed USPs

- **Extreme Passivity**: n8n/BullMQ agents handle trend/mention monitoring → AI gen → scheduling/posting without weekly logins (vs. competitors' queues needing tweaks).
- **Low/No-Tech Barrier**: One-time onboarding (OAuth + simple brand form); no dashboards to babysit — alerts via email; as easy as scrolling FB/Pinterest (ideal for plumbers/realtors/giggers).
- **Brand-Aligned AI**: Grok-tuned content gen based on user samples/tone, ensuring on-point posts (e.g., professional for realtors, casual for trainers, fun for magicians).
- **Affordable for Solos/SMBs/Giggers**: Starts at $19/mo; includes basics competitors charge extra for (analytics, agents).
- **Local Biz & Gig Optimization**: Early Google Business/FB tie-in for reviews/mentions; dynamic responses to niche trends (e.g., local events or "gig needed" queries).
- **Stability & Simplicity**: Built on MuseKit — no bloat, high reliability via queues/retries/Sentry.

### 2.4 Pricing & Packaging

Tiers mapped to MuseSocial limits (configurable no-code); free 14-day trial. Annual discounts (20%) for retention.

|Tier|Price|Features|Target|
|---|---|---|---|
|Starter|$19/mo|15 posts/mo, 5 AI gens/day, 2 platforms (e.g., FB/LinkedIn), basic alerts.|Giggers/side-hustlers (e.g., Gary the Magician).|
|Basic|$39/mo|30 posts/mo, 10 AI gens/day, 3 platforms, basic analytics/alerts.|Entry solos/SMBs (e.g., Paul the Plumber).|
|Premium|$69/mo|Unlimited posts/gens, 10 platforms, custom agents, approval queue, advanced alerts.|Power users (e.g., Rita/Carla).|

**Rationale**: Undercuts premiums (e.g., Hootsuite $99+); Starter tier draws giggers; encourages upgrades via limits. Stripe via MuseKit for easy gating.

## 3. Product Requirements & Feature Spec (PRD)

**MVP Scope**: Leverage MuseSocial for OAuth/posting/scheduling; add custom agents/onboarding.

- **Onboarding**: Wizard — connect 2–3 platforms (default FB/LinkedIn for giggers), upload brand samples/tone, set prefs (frequency, approvals); FB-simple UX.
- **Core Loop**: Agents monitor trends/mentions (n8n cron); Grok gen content; queue/post via BullMQ.
- **Dashboard**: User-facing: Post queue preview, basic engagement charts, alerts.
- **Out of Scope**: Advanced images, 10+ platforms (post-MVP).
- **Success**: Users post 5x/week passively; 90% satisfaction.

## 4. Technical Specifications (Tech Spec)

**Architecture**: Clone MuseKit; toggle MuseSocial.

- **Frontend**: Next.js dashboard — add brand form, queue viewer.
- **Backend**: Supabase DB for prefs; n8n agents for monitoring (X/FB API trends); Grok for gen; BullMQ for queues/retries.
- **Integrations**: Prioritize X/LinkedIn/Instagram/FB (prove SDKs; FB for giggers).
- **Limits**: Config via MuseSocial tiers; no-code adjustments.

- Wireframing: Visily outperformed Mokkup for our flows (smoother AI response, better form handling). Key accelerator: Detailed, context-rich prompts from Grok → standardize this. Example: Include user persona hints, exact components, mobile constraints, and benefit-oriented copy in prompts.
	- The key insight: Prompt engineering is the secret sauce when using tools like Visily. The AI doesn't "think" like a designer; it needs rich context (persona hints, layout constraints, color cues, component lists, mobile-first, benefit-oriented copy) to generate something close to what we want on the first or second try. Your quick success with the prompts proves it — without them, you'd likely spend way more time regenerating or manually adjusting.

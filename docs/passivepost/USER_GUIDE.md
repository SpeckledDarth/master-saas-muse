# PassivePost — User Guide

PassivePost is a closed-loop business intelligence platform for content creators. It combines content scheduling across 8 social platforms and 2 blog platforms, a full affiliate marketing program with 3 dashboards, connected analytics, and AI coaching powered by xAI Grok. This guide walks you through every feature step by step.

## 1. Getting Started

### First Time Setup

When you first log into PassivePost, a 4-step onboarding wizard guides you through initial setup:

1. **Welcome** -- Introduction to what PassivePost does
2. **Connect an Account** -- Link your first social media account
3. **Set Your Brand Voice** -- Tell PassivePost about your business, niche, and target audience
4. **Generate Your First Post** -- Create your first AI-generated post

After completing the wizard, you land on the Overview dashboard.

### The Overview Dashboard

The Overview page at `/dashboard/social/overview` is your home base. It displays:

- **Usage Progress Bars** -- How much of your daily and monthly limits you have used
- **Quick Stats** -- Posts created, accounts connected, AI generations used
- **Coaching Card** -- A daily tip, strategy, or motivational message generated by AI
- **Flywheel Health Score** -- A metric showing how well your content creation loop is performing
- **Quick Generate Button** -- One-click AI content creation

## 2. Setting Up Your Brand Voice

Your brand voice settings are the most important configuration in PassivePost. They tell the AI how to write content that sounds like you and your business.

### How to Set It Up

1. Go to **Brand Voice** in the sidebar (under Setup)
2. Fill in each field:

| Field | What to Enter | Example |
|-------|--------------|---------|
| **Tone** | How you want to sound | "Friendly and professional" |
| **Niche** | Your business category | "Plumbing" |
| **Location** | Where you operate | "Austin, TX" |
| **Sample URLs** | Links to your website or content you like | "https://myplumbing.com" |
| **Target Audience** | Who you are trying to reach | "Homeowners aged 30-55 in the Austin area" |
| **Posting Goals** | What you want social media to do for you | "Get more service calls and build local trust" |
| **Preferred Platforms** | Where you want to focus | Twitter, Facebook |
| **Post Frequency** | How often you want to post | "Daily" or "3 times per week" |

3. Click **Save**

### Why It Matters

Every piece of AI-generated content uses these settings. A plumber in Austin will get very different content than a photographer in New York. The more detail you provide, the better the AI performs.

### Changing Your Brand Voice

You can update your brand voice at any time. Changes take effect immediately for all future AI generations.

## 3. Connecting Social Accounts

### Supported Platforms

PassivePost supports 8 social platforms and 2 blog platforms:

**Social Platforms**: Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram, Reddit, Discord, YouTube, Pinterest

**Blog Platforms**: WordPress (REST API + App Passwords), Ghost (Admin API Key)

### How to Connect

1. Go to **Accounts** in the sidebar (under Setup)
2. Click the **Connect** button for the platform you want
3. You will be redirected to the platform's login page
4. Authorize PassivePost to manage your account
5. You will be redirected back and your account will be connected

### How Many Accounts Can I Connect?

It depends on your subscription plan:
- **Starter**: 2 platforms
- **Basic**: 3 platforms
- **Premium**: All 8 social + 2 blog platforms

### Checking Connection Status

The Accounts page shows:
- A green indicator if your account is connected and working
- The last time your credentials were validated
- Any error messages if something went wrong

### Troubleshooting Connections

If an account shows as invalid:
1. Click **Validate** to test the connection
2. If validation fails, click **Reconnect** to go through the OAuth flow again
3. Make sure you have not revoked access on the platform's own settings page

## 4. Generating AI Content

All AI content generation uses xAI Grok and pulls your real brand voice settings, niche guidance, and platform-specific constraints.

### Quick Generate (Recommended)

The fastest way to create content:

1. Click the **Quick Generate** button (floating button, bottom-right of any page)
2. A dialog opens with your AI-generated post
3. Review the content
4. Choose to:
   - **Copy** to clipboard
   - **Schedule** it for a specific date and time
   - **Post immediately**
   - **Generate again** for a different version

### From the Posts Page

1. Go to **Posts** in the sidebar
2. Click **Create Post**
3. Choose to write manually or generate with AI
4. Select the target platform(s)
5. Set a schedule or save as draft
6. Click **Save** or **Schedule**

### Tips for Better AI Content

- **Be specific in your brand voice settings** -- "Friendly plumber who cares about families" gets better results than "professional"
- **Choose the right niche** -- The niche guidance tells the AI what vocabulary and topics to use
- **Add sample URLs** -- The AI can reference your website's style
- **Include images** -- The AI can generate captions based on photos you upload (multimodal support)

### Daily AI Generation Limits

- **Starter**: 5 per day
- **Basic**: 10 per day
- **Premium**: 100 per day

## 5. Managing Posts

### The Posts Page

This is your post management hub at `/dashboard/social/posts`. You can:

- **View all posts** -- See everything you have created, sorted by date
- **Filter** -- Narrow down by platform, status, or date range
- **Search** -- Find specific posts by content
- **Bulk actions** -- Select multiple posts to delete or reschedule

### Post Statuses

| Status | What It Means |
|--------|--------------|
| **Draft** | Saved but not scheduled yet |
| **Scheduled** | Set to publish at a specific time |
| **Queued** | In the publishing queue, waiting to be sent |
| **Posted** | Successfully published to the platform |
| **Failed** | Something went wrong -- check the error message |

### Editing a Post

1. Click on any post to open the detail view
2. Make your changes
3. Click **Save**

### Deleting Posts

- Click the delete icon on any post, or
- Select multiple posts and use the bulk delete action

---

## 6. Using the Queue

### What the Queue Shows

The Queue page at `/dashboard/social/queue` displays all posts that are scheduled to be published, ordered by time. It is your "upcoming posts" view.

### Managing the Queue

- **Reorder** -- Change the order posts will go out
- **Edit** -- Make last-minute changes before publishing
- **Cancel** -- Remove a post from the queue without deleting it
- **View timing** -- See exactly when each post is scheduled to publish

### How Posts Get Published

When a post's scheduled time arrives, PassivePost automatically sends it to the platform. If the post fails (e.g., the platform is down), it retries up to 3 times before marking it as failed.

## 7. Content Calendar

### What You Will See

A month-view calendar at `/dashboard/social/calendar` with your posts plotted on each day. It gives you a bird's-eye view of your content plan.

### Calendar Features

- **Hover over a date** -- See a tooltip with how many posts are scheduled per platform
- **Click a date** -- View all posts for that day
- **Color indicators** -- Different colors for different post statuses
- **Navigate months** -- Arrow buttons to move forward and back

### Using the Calendar for Planning

The calendar helps you:
- Spot gaps in your posting schedule
- Ensure you are posting consistently
- Balance content across platforms
- Plan ahead for holidays, events, or promotions

## 8. Tracking Engagement

### The Engagement Page

Located at `/dashboard/social/engagement`, this page shows how your published posts are performing across all connected platforms.

### Metrics Tracked

| Metric | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Likes** | How many people liked or favorited your post |
| **Shares** | How many times your post was shared or retweeted |
| **Comments** | How many people replied to your post |
| **Impressions** | How many times your post was shown |
| **Reach** | How many unique people saw your post |

### Charts and Insights

- **Engagement over time** -- Line chart showing trends
- **Platform comparison** -- Bar chart comparing performance across platforms
- **Top posts** -- Your best-performing content
- **Best times** -- When your audience is most active

### How Data is Collected

PassivePost automatically pulls engagement data from your connected platforms. The default frequency is once every 24 hours, but your admin can adjust this.

## 9. Finding Leads

### The Leads Page

Located at `/dashboard/social/leads` (Basic tier and above), this page surfaces potential customers or business contacts who have shown interest through social media engagement.

### What Counts as a Lead

- Someone who comments asking about your services
- Direct messages inquiring about pricing
- Profile visits from potential customers
- Engagement patterns that suggest interest

### Using Leads

- View contact information and which platform they came from
- See their engagement history with your content
- Track lead status (new, contacted, converted)
- Filter by platform and date

## 10. Trend Alerts

### What They Are

PassivePost monitors trending topics across your connected platforms. When something relevant to your business starts trending, you get an alert.

### How to Use Trend Alerts

1. You will see trend notifications on your dashboard
2. Click on a trend to see details
3. Choose to **Generate a Post** based on the trend -- the AI will create timely, relevant content using your brand voice
4. Schedule or post the content immediately

### Why It Matters

Trending content gets more visibility. When you post about something that is already getting attention, your content is more likely to be seen and engaged with.

## 11. Bulk Importing Content

### When to Use It

If you have planned a week or month of content in advance (in a spreadsheet, notes app, etc.), you can import it all at once instead of creating posts one by one.

### How It Works

1. Go to the Posts page
2. Click **Bulk Import**
3. Provide your post data (content, platform, schedule)
4. PassivePost validates and creates all posts
5. Posts appear in your Queue and Calendar

## 12. Understanding Your Tier

### What Your Tier Controls

Your subscription tier determines your usage limits:

| | Starter | Basic | Premium |
|---|---------|-------|---------|
| Daily AI Generations | 5 | 10 | 100 |
| Daily Posts | 1 | 2 | Unlimited |
| Monthly Posts | 15 | 30 | Unlimited |
| Connected Platforms | 2 | 3 | 8 social + 2 blog |

### Checking Your Usage

- The **Overview page** shows progress bars for each limit
- Your current tier is displayed in the sidebar
- When you reach 80% of any limit, an upgrade banner appears

### What Happens at the Limit

When you hit a limit:
- You cannot create more posts or generate more AI content for that period
- Existing scheduled posts will still be published
- The limit resets at the start of the next day (daily limits) or month (monthly limits)

## 15. Tips for Best Results

### Content Strategy

1. **Post consistently** -- Regular posting builds trust over time
2. **Use the 80/20 rule** -- 80% valuable content, 20% promotional
3. **Ask questions** -- Posts with questions get more engagement
4. **Repurpose content** -- A LinkedIn article can become multiple tweets
5. **Batch-create content** -- Spend one sitting creating a week's worth of posts

### Platform Best Practices

| Platform | Best Practices |
|----------|---------------|
| **Twitter/X** | Short, punchy content. Use hashtags sparingly. Engage in threads. |
| **LinkedIn** | Professional tone. Share industry insights. Use storytelling. |
| **Facebook** | Mix of content types. Community-focused. Share local content. |
| **Instagram** | Visual-first. Strong captions. Relevant hashtags. |
| **Reddit** | Value-driven. Engage authentically. Follow subreddit rules. |
| **YouTube** | Consistent uploads. Strong titles and thumbnails. |
| **Pinterest** | High-quality images. Descriptive pin text. Vertical format. |
| **Discord** | Community engagement. Conversational tone. |

### AI Generation Tips

1. **Refine your brand voice** -- The more detail you provide, the better the AI writes
2. **Edit AI content** -- Use it as a starting point, then add your personal touch
3. **Try different tones** -- Experiment with casual vs. professional to see what resonates
4. **Include images** -- Upload photos for the AI to create better captions
5. **Use trend alerts** -- Timely content gets more engagement

### Tier-Specific Tips

**Starter Tier:**
- Check your **Engagement page** regularly to learn which posts perform best
- Set up your **Brand Voice** thoroughly -- the AI quality difference between minimal and detailed settings is significant
- Write **one blog post per week** and use Repurpose to generate social snippets from it

**Basic Tier:**
- Run **Calendar Autopilot** at the start of each week to let AI plan your schedule
- Check **Topic Fatigue** weekly and pivot to underused content themes when needed
- Use **Batch Repurpose** to convert multiple drafts to new platform formats at once
- Run the **Gig Lead Scanner** daily for fresh leads

**Premium Tier:**
- Set up the **Client Approval Portal** for freelance or agency work
- Review your **Monthly Report Card** to demonstrate value to clients
- Use **Competitor Gap Analysis** monthly to find content opportunities
- Enable **Content Recycling** for evergreen posts
- Export **White-Label Reports** for client meetings

## 16. Blog Publishing

PassivePost supports publishing to WordPress (via REST API) and Ghost (via Admin API Key).

### How to Use Blog Publishing

1. **Connect a Blog Platform** -- Go to Blog Home from the sidebar. Click "Connect" and choose WordPress or Ghost. Enter your API credentials.
2. **Write a Blog Post** -- Click "Compose" to open the Markdown editor. Write your article, add a title and excerpt, and use the SEO preview to see how it will appear in search results.
3. **Publish** -- Choose which connected platforms to publish to and click Publish. The post will be sent to all selected platforms.
4. **Repurpose to Social** -- After publishing, click "Repurpose" to have AI generate 5-7 social media snippets from your blog post. Each snippet links back to the original article, creating a traffic flywheel.
5. **Schedule Snippets** -- Click "Schedule All" to spread the snippets across the next 7-14 days for sustained promotion.

## 17. Content Intelligence

PassivePost includes AI-powered content analysis tools accessible from the Intelligence page (under Insights in the sidebar). Available on Basic tier and above.

- **Content Grader** -- Before publishing, get an AI score (0-100) on engagement potential, readability, and brand alignment
- **Content DNA** -- See what patterns your best-performing posts share (tone, length, topic, timing)
- **Topic Fatigue** -- Get warned when you are posting too much about the same topic
- **Content Mix** -- See if your content balance is right (educational vs promotional vs entertaining)
- **Tone Drift** -- Get alerted if your writing style is drifting from your configured brand voice
- **Engagement Prediction** -- See predicted engagement metrics before you publish
- **Content Briefs** -- AI generates detailed briefs with title, talking points, audience segment, format, timing, and hashtags

## 18. Automation

PassivePost automates repetitive content marketing tasks. Available on Basic tier and above from the Automation page in the sidebar.

- **Calendar Autopilot** -- AI fills your content calendar for the week based on optimal timing and content mix
- **Content Recycling** -- High-performing old posts are automatically identified for reposting
- **Batch Repurpose** -- Convert multiple posts into different formats at once
- **Evergreen Finder** -- Identifies content that stays relevant over time
- **Blog-to-Thread** -- Converts blog posts into Twitter/X thread format with one click
- **Cross-Post Timing** -- Staggers posts across platforms for maximum reach
- **Repurpose Chains** -- Multi-step content transformation pipelines saved as reusable templates
- **Draft Warnings** -- Get notified about drafts sitting unpublished
- **Content Decay Alerts** -- Get notified when previously high-performing content starts losing engagement

## 24. AI Voice Fine-Tuner

Teach PassivePost your unique writing style:

1. Go to the Brand Voice page
2. Find the "Voice Fine-Tuner" section
3. Paste 3-15 of your best writing samples
4. Click "Analyze My Voice"
